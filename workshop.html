WORKSHOP OBJECTIVES

The workshop is geared towards fostering novel relationships among data providers, users, and beneficiaries of Earth observation data within the CMS4D project. Our objectives include:

  1. Involve natural resource managers, especially from federal, regional and local conservation units and protected areas, and landowners, who may be interested in the products that will be developed by the CMS4D project, as a way of supporting the use of these products for the sustainable development and conservation of the Cerrado.
  2. Extend the dialog between the project's end users, collaborators and scientists and we will seek to (1) solicit ideas about which CMS4D resources and products are most useful for fuels management; (2) identify the needs of land managers to quantify both the desired fuel load condition and the deviation from the desired condition in terms of AGC stocks; (3) foster long-term relationships between the project team and land managers; (4) promote a sense of ownership or buy-in from managers in the development and deployment process; (5) increase the usability of CMS4D; (6) educate end users on how to use CMS4D.

EXPECTED GOALS

Outlined below is a list of potential products that may emerge from the seminar and working group sessions. These products will be drafted during the event and subsequently refined based on the outcomes of the group activities:

  1. A descriptive summary of the ideas proposed by natural resource managers, especially federal, regional and local conservation units and protected areas, and landowners, for incorporation into the CMS4D tool.
  2. Identifying potential leaders and collaborators to take part in the next phases of the project and establishing active communication channels to facilitate collaboration.
  3. Identify the demands for knowledge and training to use the tools and products that will be produced by the CMS4D project.
